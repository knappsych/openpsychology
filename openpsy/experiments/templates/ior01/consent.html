Thanks for considering participating in {{ pretty_name }}.

Purpose:

This experiment was designed to see if a classical experimental result in participants' response time to different stimuli can be reproduced in an online setting using only javascript. If it can, that sets the stage for designing other response time dependent online experiments to offer instructors and students a wider range of experiments to participate in online. It would also suggest that new experimental hypotheses could be offered on line when traditional experimental facilities are lacking or when a wider range of participants is desired.

What can you expect:

This experiment contains two stages. In the first stage you'll be asked to provide basic demographic information (e.g. age and gender). In the experimental stage you'll see two grey boxes to the left and the right of a fixation cross that you should focus on through the duration of the experiment. Your task is to respond as quickly as possible by clicking anywhere on the screen when you see a small grey square appear in the center of one of the boxes. Additional instructions and example stimuli will be provided should you consent to participate.

Once you begin the experiment, you should be able to complete it within about 5-10 minutes. Please, do not begin the experiment unless you expect to be free from distractions for the duration of the experiment.

Should you agree to participate, you'll be sent an email with a code you'll need to use to access the actual experiment. This is to verify that you are who you say you are.

Your rights:

You have the right to discontinue participating at any time. For each of the demographic questions, you'll have options to not respond, if you're uncomfortable providing that information. No data will be saved until you've completed the experiment. Thus if you wish to discontinue at any time, you can shut the browser window and move on to other activities.

You also have the right to receive more information about this study. If you would like more information before consenting, please contact the study's primary investigator using the inquiry form.

You also have the right to learn more about the specific hypotheses under investigation in this experiment. At the end of your participation you'll receive a debriefing email that will describe some of the specific hypotheses and where you can learn more if you're interested.

If you believe that any of your rights are violated during the course of the experiment, you have the opportunity to contact the chair of the Institutional Review Board or equivalent of the institution who has approved the primary investigator to conduct this research.

Potential risks:

There are few anticipated risks to participating. It's possible that you might find some of the task repetitive or boring, but it's also possible that you'll find participating to be an enjoyable experience. If you treat the experiment like a game and try to respond as quickly as possible, you should have a more enjoyable experience and provide the researcher with better data.

One potential risk is that your identity could be linked to your individual responses. Several steps have been taken to ensure that the probability this could occur are minimized. First, your email address is not stored with the data you submit, or even with which studies you participate in. Your emails are temporarily stored during your participation in order to send you debriefing information that can be used to verify that you participated. Your email address will also be stored separately from any other data to contact you should there be the opportunity for follow-up studies or other research opportunities. These emails are double password-protected, which provides roughly the same level of protection that signing a consent form that's stored in a locked cabinet in a locked room provides.

Everyone will have access to the raw data from which your personally identifying information will have been removed. If you're interested, you can download the data and perform your own analyses.

Potential benefits:

If you are a college student, some of your professors might offer you extra-credit for participating. You can share the debriefing email with them as evidence that you successfully completed the experiment. Alternatively, you could take a screenshot at the end of the experiment that indicates you're finished. Or, if you leave the experiment and later want evidence, you'll receive a page that indicates that you already participate, if you reenter your email in this consent form.

Although no direct financial incentives are provided for participating in this experiment, your participation will be useful in answering important questions about human performance, preferences, and experiences. By participating, you'll also gain first-hand experience about what the research process is like. This can be useful in deciding whether you might be interested in pursuing a research career. You also might find that you're particularly interested in some topic. As this experiment is being conducted solely online, there's always the possibility that you could identify a question of your own that you could investigate using this platform. Such inquiries should be directed to Open Psychology using our contact form or to the primary investigator using the experiment inquiry form.

Your responsibilities:

Although your participation is completely voluntary, it's important that you take this experience seriously and attempt to perform as well as you can. Such conscientious participation, will help to ensure the validity of our study and be of greater scientific value. As mentioned before, if at any point you prefer to discontinue participation, please do so, as opposed to participating without doing your best.

In participating you agree that:

  • Your participation is voluntary and not coerced.
  • You are 18, older, or have your primary caregiver's permission to participate.
  • You understand what you'll be asked to do in the experiment.
  • You understand the potential risks and benefits of participating.
  • To ensure the integrity of the experiment, you will not discuss any of the specific questions or hypotheses related to this experiment with anyone who has not yet participated in the experiment.

If you agree to participate, please enter your email. If you agree to be contacted for follow-up studies or other research opportunities, please check the "Permission to contact" box.

Please check your email for the access code, should you agree to participate. If you don't receive the access code promptly, please check your spam and trash folders. If you still can't find it, please contact Open Psychology.
