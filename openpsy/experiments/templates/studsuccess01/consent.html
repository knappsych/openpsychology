Thanks for considering participating in {{ pretty_name }}.

Purpose:

This experiment was designed to explore whether or not your previous and current financial experiences and financial preferences are related to academic success. This is an important topic to study as indications of risk to attaining academic success, could be used to offer students additional services that could help them succeed (e.g. peer-accountability groups or specific skills workshops.

What can you expect:

This experiment contains several stages. In one stage you'll be asked to provide basic demographic information (e.g. age and gender). In another stage you'll be asked to indicate how much you agree with 20 statements about your current and previous financial experiences (e.g. while growing up, my caregivers regularly took us out for pizza and / or other treats). All of these questions will be general in nature. Thus, you won't be asked to divulge your actual income or savings. In another stage you'll be asked to indicate which of two options you would prefer (e.g. 20$ today or 40$ in one week).

If you provide an academic email (e.g. yourname@university.edu), the experimenter will contact your registrar's office to access your current and future academic records. The data sought include ACT/SAT scores, high school GPA, high school rank, college GPA, number of credits attempted, number of credits completed, time to degree, and records related to academic suspension and dropping out. These data will be stripped of any direct personally identifying information. Additionally, these data will be transmitted and stored securely. By providing an academic email address, you are giving the experimenter explicit permission to access your academic records.

If you don't attend a university, you may still participate as we're also interested in determining whether one's financial experiences predict one's financial preferences.

Once you begin the experiment, you should be able to complete it within about 15-20 minutes. Please, do not begin the experiment unless you expect to be free from distractions for the duration of the experiment.

Should you agree to participate, you'll be sent an email with a code you'll need to use to access the actual experiment. This is to verify that you are who you say you are.

Your rights:

You have the right to discontinue participating at any time. For some of the demographic questions, you'll have options to not respond, if you're uncomfortable providing that information. There are no such options to not respond to the questions regarding financial experiences and preferences, so if you wish to discontinue, just shut the close the webpage. No data will be saved until you've completed the experiment.

You also have the right to receive more information about this study. If you would like more information before consenting, please contact the study's primary investigator using the inquiry form.

You also have the right to learn more about the specific hypotheses under investigation in this experiment. At the end of your participation you'll receive a debriefing email that will describe some of the specific hypotheses and where you can learn more if you're interested.

If you believe that any of your rights are violated during the course of the experiment, you have the opportunity to contact the chair of the Institutional Review Board or equivalent of the institution who has approved the primary investigator to conduct this research.

Potential risks:

There are few anticipated risks to participating. It's possible that you might find some of the questions repetitive or boring, but it's also possible that you'll find participating to be an enjoyable experience.

One potential risk is that your identity could be linked to your individual responses. Several steps have been taken to ensure that the probability this could occur are minimized. First, your email address is not stored with the data you submit, or even with which studies you participate in. Your emails are temporarily stored during your participation in order to send you debriefing information that can be used to verify that you participated. Your email address will also be stored separately from any other data to contact you should there be the opportunity for follow-up studies or other research opportunities. These emails are double password-protected, which provides roughly the same level of protection that signing a consent form that's stored in a locked cabinet in a locked room provides.

In collecting data from your institutions about your academic performance, your personally identifying information (e.g. name or address) will be removed from the data or transformed so the experimenter can connect your experimental data with your academic performance. The experimenters will obtain these data manually, so no sensitive information is exposed over the internet, or have the data encrypted and/or password protected before being electronically submitted.

Only the experimenters or their assistants will have access to the raw data. Should the results be presented publicly, the data will be presented in aggregate form with no information that could be used to identify an individual with their performance.

Potential benefits:

If you are a college student, some of your professors might offer you extra-credit for participating. You can share the debriefing email with them as evidence that you successfully completed the experiment.

Although no direct financial incentives are provided for participating in this experiment, your participation will be useful in answering important questions about human performance, preferences, and experiences. By participating, you'll also gain first-hand experience about what the research process is like. This can be useful in deciding whether you might be interested in pursuing a research career. You also might find that you're particularly interested in some topic. As this experiment is being conducted solely online, there's always the possibility that you could identify a question of your own that you could investigate using this platform. Such inquiries should be directed to Open Psychology using our contact form or to the primary investigator using the experiment inquiry form.

Your responsibilities:

Although your participation is completely voluntary, it's important that you take this experience seriously. For the questions concerning your financial experiences and preferences, the right answers are the honest ones. Please consider each statement or option carefully before responding. Such conscientious participation, will help to ensure the validity of our study and be of greater scientific value. As mentioned before, if at any point you prefer to discontinue participation, please do so, as opposed to participating without thoughtfully considering the options.

In participating you agree that:

  • Your participation is voluntary and not coerced.
  • You are 18, older, or have your primary caregiver's permission to participate.
  • You understand what you'll be asked to do in the experiment.
  • You understand the potential risks and benefits of participating.
  • If you are a student, you agree that the experimenters may contact your educational institution to attempt to obtain information about your academic success.
  • If you are a student, your participation, in no way obligates your institution to divulge any information about your academic success.
  • To ensure the integrity of the experiment, you will not discuss any of the specific questions or hypotheses related to this experiment with anyone who has not yet participated in the experiment.
  • If you provide an academic email address, you grant the experimenter explicit permission to access to your current and future academic records from the institution identified in the email.

If you agree to participate, please enter your email. If you are currently a student, please provide your university email address so the experimenters can attempt to obtain measures related to your student success. If you agree to be contacted for follow-up studies or other research opportunities, please leave the "Permission to contact" box checked. Otherwise, uncheck the box and we won't contact you except to send you the access code and debriefing information.

Please check your email for the access code, should you agree to participate. If you don't receive the access code promptly, please check your spam and trash folders. If you still can't find it, please contact Open Psychology.
